# v1.12.1

A follow-up to the big **loans and mortgages** release, focused on making it accurate and finished. The overpayment simulator gains **goal-seek** ("how much extra to save $X of interest, or be paid off by this month?") and a **scenario comparison chart**, and a cluster of fixes makes loan **interest match your bank** — even for accounts that started before their configured date, or where an overpayment and an installment land on the same day. This release also adds a de-identified **support backup** so you can attach real-looking data to a bug report without sharing your finances, rebuilds the **Monthly Totals** chart to be time-aware, sharpens **PDF exports** (proper diacritics, much smaller files), and fixes a handful of import and balance-chart rough edges.

## Loans & Mortgages

### Work backwards from a goal

The overpayment simulator now does **goal-seek**: instead of guessing an extra amount and reading off the result, tell it the result you want and it solves for the payment. Two modes — **"save $X of interest"** and **"be paid off by this month"** — each finding the recurring extra per payment that gets you there. The solved amount drops straight into the simulator (no separate Apply step), so the schedule, chart, and savings cards all recompute at once. Targets you've already met report zero needed; ones you can't reach say so. (Thanks **@WMP**.)

### Compare your saved scenarios on one chart

Saved overpayment scenarios can now be drawn together as **arcs on a shared timeline** — each arc spans from today to that scenario's payoff date, and its height is the interest it saves versus paying the minimum. Narrower and taller is better, at a glance. The legend is interactive, the tooltip reports each scenario's real interest saved and payoff date, the chart **exports to PNG**, and the saved-scenarios panel picked up a **CSV / PDF export** (the PDF bundles headline cards, the chart, and the comparison table). The chart appears once you've saved more than one scenario. (Thanks **@WMP**.)

### Loan interest that matches your bank

Three fixes make the schedule, cards, and reports agree with your statements:

- **Interest is counted by category, regardless of date.** Everything on the loan detail derives from your payment history, which used to hide any activity before the loan's configured start date — so a migrated mortgage's earlier history, or an interest-only grace period, silently vanished. Separately-booked interest is already scoped to the loan by its interest category and source account, so that date floor is gone: your full history shows and counts. (For sequential refinances that reused one interest category, the guidance is now to give each refinance its own category.) (Thanks **@WMP**.)
- **No more invented interest on a principal-only payment.** When a loan books interest as its own expense, a payment with no paired interest (for example, an overpayment and a regular installment on the same day) was being assigned an *estimated* interest figure — inflating cumulative interest and everything derived from it. Those payments now carry zero, since the date's real interest is already recorded in the booked expenses. (Thanks **@WMP**.)
- **The loan reports match the detail page.** The Loan Amortization and Debt Payoff Timeline reports now load the same separately-booked interest the loan detail page uses, so they no longer show a different interest total for the same loan. (Thanks **@WMP**.)

### Rate history and simulator polish

- The **Rate History** panel now scrolls inside its card instead of stretching the page when you've recorded many rate changes, and it **always sits full-width above the Overpayment Simulator** — previously its position flipped depending on the order you interacted with it. (Thanks **@WMP** for the scroll fix.)
- The past-impact baseline now starts at your **earliest actual payment** rather than a stale configured start date, so there's no phantom segment before the real history begins. (Thanks **@WMP**.)
- A failed saved-scenarios or rate-history load no longer **fails silently** — you get a clear toast and a "Try again", instead of an empty panel that made a saved scenario look deleted (and then collided with a duplicate-name error when you re-saved it). (Thanks **@WMP**.)

## Support Backup

### Share a bug report without sharing your finances

A new **Create Support Backup** action (Settings → Help & Support) produces a **de-identified copy of your data** you can safely attach to a GitHub issue. It's an ordinary backup file that restores through the normal flow into a throwaway instance — nothing new to install to open it. Names are masked, free text and secrets (descriptions, notes, memos, account numbers, API keys) are dropped, and your private amounts are scaled by a single hidden multiplier while public values that would give it away — FX rates, security prices, interest rates — stay intact; every UUID is remapped so the file can't be correlated with your account. The engine works as a strict **allowlist**, so a future database column can't silently start leaking. You can scope it to specific accounts, protect it with a password, and preview the before/after first. It's de-identification, not anonymity — dates, frequencies, and structure survive on purpose, so bugs still reproduce. (Thanks **@WMP**.)

## Transactions

### A time-aware Monthly Totals chart

The **Monthly Totals** bar chart on the transactions page has been rebuilt. It used to plot one equal-width bar per month that had activity, so an irregular payment series looked evenly spaced and the average divided by *active* months rather than time elapsed. Now it rolls up to an **adaptive Month / Quarter / Year granularity** (auto-selected by span, with a manual toggle), **gap-fills empty periods** so bars are spaced by real elapsed time, labels the axis and tooltip by period, supports **drill-down by bucket**, and averages over elapsed periods. Clipped axis labels and a count that could lag the chart after an edit are fixed too.

### More time-period filters

The transactions filter gains **All Dates**, **Last 30 Days**, and **Last 365 Days**, filling the gaps between the existing presets — fully translated across all locales.

### Balance history across your whole account

The balance history chart gains an **all-time** mode spanning your account's full history instead of the default one-year window, kept fast by **server-side downsampling** (thinned to at most 400 points while always keeping the latest). The chart shows the covered date range and switches its axis between month and year labels as the span grows; the transactions page uses it whenever no start-date filter is set, with a banner for all-zero (e.g. closed) accounts.

### Liability accounts read correctly

For credit cards, loans, mortgages, and lines of credit, a **negative balance is normal**, so the balance history chart no longer flags the minimum with a warning marker or a "Lowest" alarm — it uses a neutral "Min Balance" label while still colouring every figure by sign.

## Import

### Microsoft Money voided transactions

QIF import now understands **Microsoft Money's voided transactions**, which Money exports as $0.00 rows with a `VOID ` payee prefix and the amount dropped. Monize strips the prefix (so it doesn't create separate "VOID …" payees), marks the transaction void, and adds a note explaining the original amount was lost in the export. (Closes #914.)

### Land on the account you just imported

After a file import, **View Transactions** and **View Investments** now take you to the account(s) you *just imported* — single, bulk, or multi-account QIF — instead of falling back to the last account you happened to view. Imports into closed accounts stay visible too. (Closes #911.)

## Reports & PDF Export

### Cleaner, smaller PDFs

- **Diacritics render correctly.** The built-in PDF font was Western-only, so Polish `ł`, Cyrillic, Greek, and some currency symbols came out garbled. Exports now embed a Roboto subset for all text, including tables. (Thanks **@WMP**.)
- **Files are much smaller.** Chart images were embedded uncompressed — one loan report was around 33 MB. They're now compressed, cutting that to a small fraction with no visible loss, and long table cells wrap instead of overflowing. (Thanks **@WMP**.)
- Fixed a bug where a chart PDF could come out **near-blank** because a tiny legend icon was captured instead of the chart itself. (Closes #886.)

## Bug Fixes

- **Backup filenames are dated by your timezone preference**, not UTC or the browser's timezone, so the date in the filename matches your calendar. (Closes #912.)
- Opening a transaction, account, or scheduled-transaction form no longer briefly **flashes raw UUIDs** in the payee, category, institution, and asset-category fields while their option lists load. (Closes #925.)
- **Account type names** (e.g. "Chequing") now always appear in your chosen language, including on the import screens, instead of occasionally leaking the raw type. (Thanks **@bcorob**. Closes #897.)

## Under the Hood

- Database migration `099` records the recurring-extra **payment frequency** on saved loan scenarios, backing the simulator's goal-seek.
- A **Roboto font subset** is bundled for PDF export, and `docs/support-backup.md` documents the new support backup.
